Paragon Traits

Paragon Fortitude.

The creature has multiple pools of hit points, each of which is tracked separately. All damage and healing must be completely applied to only one pool. When a pool is reduced to zero, all ongoing conditions and effects affecting the creature end. Once a pool is reduced to zero, that pool cannot receive any healing until after a long rest. If all hit of the point pools are reduced to zero, the creature is killed.

Paragon Exhaustion.

The creature may take one complete turn in each round of combat for each hit point pool it has above zero and receives one reaction between each of its turns. When a pool of hit points has been reduced to zero, the creature loses one turn each round thereafter. The creature determines initiative normally for its first turn, though it gains advantage on the roll. Each subsequent turn is inserted immediately after any one PC’s turn in the initiative order.

Brambles.

A creature within 5 feet of a patch of brambles must succeed on a DC 13 Strength saving throw or be restrained by the brambles for 1d4 rounds. A creature restrained by the brambles can use its action to make a Strength check (DC 13). On a success, it frees itself. The shadow beast can't use this lair action until it has used a different one.

Silent Image.

An illusory shadow beast appears in the lair, functioning as a silent image spell. The image may move on the shadow beast's initiative count. No other lair actions may be taken while the image exists, but the shadow beast may dispel the illusion at any time.

Darkness.

If the shadow beast is below half health, the shadows in the shadow beast's lair grow until the entire area is affected by the darkness spell. The shadow beast is unaffected by this magical darkness. This lair action may only be taken once per day.
